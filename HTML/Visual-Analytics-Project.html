Visual Analytics Project

Project Scope

With increased climate change driven by unsustainable practices, Singapore must do its best to cultivate sustainable habits in all aspects of its system. As such, the Singapore Green Plan 2030 came to be, aimed at advancing Singapore’s national agenda on sustainable development.


My team's goal in this project is to determine whether the hypothesis “Singapore is committed to implementing a sustainable future.” is true.

Role and Solution

My team created a visualisation report regarding Singapore's efforts in sustainable development to enable our stakeholders know more on Singapore's sustainability development. I was assigned to work on data relating to Sustainable Living - one of the aspects of the Green Plan.


When creating a visualisation report, it is essential to determine its main target audience and their needs. As such, we started by researching on our respective Green Pillars to gain insights on the possible questions we may ask sustainability-related stakeholders. Afterwards, the questions were sent out, either via surveys or interviews.


After obtaining the relevent business requirements, we collected various datasets, which are then used in the data cleaning and data visualisation process. A report was written to provide an analysis on the visuals created, including conclusions made by each team member.

Work Process

Here is the flow of the project:


  1. Research and listing of possible datasets
  2. Conduct surveys + interviews
  3. Data preparation, either in Excel or Power BI
  4. Modelling in Power BI
  5. Create visuals

Afterwards, the visuals had to be integrated into a single Power BI Report. As such, my team set time slots for each member to load their visuals into the main PBIX file. We helped one another by improving one another's visuals when needed.
