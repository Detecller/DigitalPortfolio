UX Design

Project Scope

My team was assigned to design and develop an interactive prototype for a hotel website. Languages used include HTML, CSS and JavaScript (JS).

Role and Solution

As the team leader of this project, I was responsible for delegating the work in the group, ensuring work gets done by the deadline. It was decided that I would be working on the Home and Events webpages, followed by the top navigation, menu and footer of the website.


To build a hotel website which is capable of capturing the attention of viewers and encouraging them to visit the hotel, guidelines relating to UX design have to be considered, such as Nielsen Norman's 10 Usability Heuristics, the 7 UX Design Principles, amongst others.

Work Process

As a student who had limited experience in website building via code, I had to learn on the go. While the learning process was arduous, with many trial-and-errors, I managed to pick up essential skills relating to HTML, CSS and JS, and implement them with UX (User Experience) design theories to build a fantastic hotel website.


Here is the general flow which my team used in building our webpages:


  1. Decide on purpose of webpage
  2. Note down UX theories which can be used
  3. Develop wireframe of prototype
  4. Coding of webpage with UX in mind

After the webpages were completed, integration had to be done, where the webpages were merged together in a single directory. This includes the modification of codes to ensure the webpages were compatible with one another.
